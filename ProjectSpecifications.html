Final Project Specifications

Sanjeev Kumar

Apr 5, 2017

We have reached the end of our journey through the world of Big Data Advanced Analytics. Before we finish though - we have the final project. This project, like most of the work we have done in the course, will focus on solving business problems through data analytics, specifically Machine Learning Algorithms.

What is the project about?

We are all working for the consulting company BIG Data Exploration and Analysis Llc (aka BIG DEAL Consulting) for this project. Managing partner for Big Deal Consulting - Sanjeev Kumar - has just sold an exciting new project to an online dating platform OkMatch.com. The clint is really interested in finding out if they can successfully match people in their platform using advanced analytics methodologies. They have collected data on people going through speed dating and asking Big Deal Consulting to build a model of predicting whether two individuals will match with each other or not. Now it is your job to execute and deliver on this project.

What are you supposed to do?

You are expected to take the role of business consultants. The client has given you all the data that they have and they expect you to do your magic with the data and get back to them with some actionable business insights that you discover from the data. Your output, at the very least, will include a model that will predict matches between people. It is a classification problem - each obervation needs to be classified as Yes or No.

But what are we to do specifically?

That is up to you. It essentially boils down to - ask good questions and provide good answers to those questions. The client expects you to carefully consider what business questions can be asked and answered with the data provided. You should explore the data, generate appropriate visualizations and summary, exploratory statistics to better understand the data, carefully design your hypothesis and business questions that you think you can answer through the data and then proceed to conduct the analysis to actually answer the questions. After the analysis you should devote sufficient effort to communicate your results and interpret your output to a general audience. You have a completely blank slate - you decide what questions to ask and you decide how to answer them. The basic task is to predict whether two people will be a match or not. You should feel free to extend the scope of the project beyond this basic task.

What data do you have?

You have the raw data available to you in a file named Speed Dating Data.csv. Details of what you have in this file are available in a Data Key document named Speed Dating Data Key.doc. As the data is quite messy, a sample data cleaning script is available for you to use - the script is named DataCleaningScript.R. Please note that this cleaning script is only a suggestion and you should feel free to edit or change this script if you feel that parts of it are not appropriate.

What are your deliverables?

You will need to provide two specific deliverables:

  1. A project report (essentially an RMD file and its HTML output) that shows your work. The project report should be written for the top management of the client company - hence should not be overtly technical. You should use visualization techniques liberally to communicate better with the non-technical audience.

  2. A small pitch presentation document that you can deliver in 5 mins flat. Top management of the client can only give you five mins of their time and you need to be able communicate your results in that time.

  3. An actual presentation made in class to a panel of judges. Given the paucity of time, only one person from the group (the lead consultant, chosen by the project group) should deliver the presentation.

  4. (Extra Credit) An R-Shiny web dashboard that presents interesting ways to interact with the data.
